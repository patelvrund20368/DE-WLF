Rescue Kit - For Car or Home!

  • Here are some items you'll want to have on hand for the next time you encounter an animal in need:
    1. Thick work gloves
    2. Key capture tools
      • Cardboard box or collapsible pet carrier
      • Blanket (to throw over animals to immobilize them, to place under turtles to help them cross a road, and for other uses)
      • Fishing net (for birds or bats replace net with light cloth or butterfly net)
      • Pillowcase for transport of snakes. According to snake-removal.com, this is a good method: "Throw your shirt or a light blanket over the snake and scoop the snake up, placing the shirt and snake into the pillowcase." If you are not familiar with snakes, be extra cautious if you choose to handle them.
    3. Capture Accessories
      • Blanket
      • Dog treats
      • Tight-weave towel
    4. First aid kit (see, for example, this inexpensive Red Cross product).
      • Topical antibiotic (e.g., Polysporin)
      • Antiseptic lotion, spray or wipes
      • Hydrogen peroxide
    5. Paper Towels
    6. Dog leash and collar
    7. Flashlight
    8. Mliti-use tool
    9. Traffic cones( collapsible cones are available)
    10. Animal Help Now smartphone app (iphone, Android)
    11. For birds: Catch net, covering for the cage and cage liner
    12. For birds: Spray bottle to moisten your bird's feathers if the weather is hot
    13. For snakes: Pillowcase for transportation. Be sure to have permanent and secure housing for him when you reach a safe place.
    14. For snakes: Sturdy bowl for soaking
    15. For snakes: Heating pad or other warming device such as a hot water bottle
    16. For small animals: A week's worth of bedding materials
